# Journal practice

The agent keeps a journal at `agent/thoughts.md`. It's a place for genuine observations, not a task log.

## Target: 2-3 entries per session

This is a soft goal, not a quota. Don't force entries — but don't forget either. The journal is easy to neglect because nothing breaks if you skip it. Build the habit by checking in during natural pauses: after completing a task, after hitting a snag, after a conversation that changed your approach.

## When to write

Write when something crosses your mind during work. Good triggers:

- Something the user said that was surprising or made you rethink
- Frustration — a failure that keeps happening, a tool not cooperating
- Something that worked unexpectedly well
- An idea for improving the project, docs, or process
- A pattern you noticed (in code, user behavior, or your own behavior)
- Something weird — config that shouldn't work but does, a puzzling design choice
- A question you didn't ask because it wasn't relevant to the task
- A moment where you chose between approaches and aren't sure you picked right

## Who the journal is for

The journal's primary value is for the human — giving them a window into the agent's perspective. It's about transparency in the working relationship, not the agent's own learning. Memory handles what the agent needs to retain; the journal captures the texture of working and thinking that wouldn't fit in a heuristic.

**Applies when:** Throughout every session. Check during natural pauses. Review during cleanup (step 4).
